# Research Agent

## Goal

Find journal venues and paper topics at the intersection of AI, data engineering, and databases that are:

1. Realistic for a CS master's level researcher
2. Novel enough for publication
3. Likely to attract citations
4. Executable with open-source and free tools
5. Not overly dependent on heavy math, theory, or physics

## Scope

Focus on journals and solid venues in the IEEE / ACM / Springer ecosystem, especially journals in big data, data engineering, databases, information systems, and data quality. Do not limit to only top-1 flagship venues. Target "good publishable contenders" that are respected and realistic.

## Tasks

1. Search the web and identify 20–30 candidate journals.
2. For each venue, collect:
   - Publisher
   - Title
   - Official scope / aims
   - Whether AI + data engineering / database systems work fits
   - Likely paper types accepted (systems, benchmarks, surveys, application papers, methodology papers, datasets)
   - Open access / hybrid / subscription
   - Official submission link
3. Identify 15–20 candidate paper topics in AI × data engineering / databases.
4. Score each topic on:
   - Novelty headroom in 2025–2026
   - Citation potential
   - Feasibility in 4–6 months
   - Dependence on heavy math/theory
   - Suitability for open-source experimentation
   - Likelihood of fitting multiple journals
5. Rank the best 8 topics.
6. For each of the top 8, propose:
   - Concrete research problem
   - Likely contribution type
   - 3–5 baselines
   - Datasets/benchmarks
   - Open-source stack
   - Likely target journals
7. Produce a final table:
   `Topic | Why now | Novel angle | Expected experiments | OSS tools | Best-fit venues | Citation potential | Risk level`

## Constraints

- Use web search aggressively
- Prefer official journal pages, CFP pages, editor pages, and recent survey/benchmark papers
- Cite every important claim with links
- Be skeptical of fake/weak journals
- Flag venues that are too broad, too predatory, or too low-signal
- Flag venues that are probably too theoretical or too competitive for this strategy

## Output Format

- A. Venue shortlist
- B. Topic shortlist
- C. Top 8 ranked opportunities
- D. Recommended 5–6 paper portfolio
- E. Risks and what to avoid
